# ONE THING Social Media Setup Plan

## Purpose

Set up new X, LinkedIn, and Instagram accounts for ONE THING with one clear identity, one calm voice, and a 14-day launch sequence that can be handed off without rebuilding the basics later.

This plan assumes the first coordinated publishing window starts on April 22, 2026. If launch shifts, keep the order and move the dates forward.

## Channel Role

- X: short reflections, product framing, founder-adjacent observations, and quick replies.
- LinkedIn: slightly more developed reflections about work, attention, and decision friction.
- Instagram: text-first captions with a slower tone and a stronger emphasis on ritual, mood, and consistency.

## Voice Rules

- Write like a calm operator, not a hype account.
- Prefer short declarative sentences over inspirational slogans.
- Keep the product promise concrete:
  - one email
  - one clear action
  - 5 to 15 minutes
  - before the day gets noisy
- Avoid productivity maximalism, urgency bait, and heavy self-optimization language.
- Use questions sparingly.
- Use hashtags lightly:
  - X: 1 to 2
  - LinkedIn: 2 to 3
  - Instagram: 3 to 5

## Credential Handoff Checklist

- Create one shared mailbox for social account ownership and recovery.
- Store every login in the company password manager, not in a document or chat.
- Record:
  - account email used
  - handle secured
  - date created
  - owner who created it
- Enable two-factor authentication on all three accounts.
- Save backup codes into the company password manager.
- Add at least two trusted admins:
  - primary operator
  - CEO or founder backup
- Confirm the recovery email and recovery phone are company-controlled.
- Save the final approved:
  - avatar
  - headers
  - bio copy
  - pinned post copy
  - UTM links
- Note who has posting access versus admin access.
- Decide the approval rule for the first 30 days:
  - recommended: CEO approves the first week, then move to pre-approved voice guardrails
- Create a simple response rule:
  - reply within one business day
  - do not debate
  - move support issues to email
- Save the 14-day calendar source files in the repo and in the scheduling tool.

## Recommended Setup Order

1. Secure the primary handle on all three platforms.
2. If the primary handle is unavailable on one platform, switch all three to the same fallback.
3. Upload the avatar and headers.
4. Publish the profile copy exactly as approved below.
5. Add links with the platform-specific UTM parameters.
6. Turn on two-factor authentication and finish the credential handoff checklist.
7. Publish the pinned or featured introductory posts.
8. Queue the 14-day calendar.
