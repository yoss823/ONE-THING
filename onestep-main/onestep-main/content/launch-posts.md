## Post 3 — Reddit

**Subreddit:** r/productivity (primary) or r/selfimprovement (secondary)
**Type:** New post (not a comment)
**Suggested title:** "I got frustrated with every productivity app doing too much, so I built something that does almost nothing"

---

I've tried most of the big productivity apps. The problem was never a missing feature — it was that every system eventually demanded more attention than the work itself.

The specific moment I kept hitting: opening everything in the morning and then losing 20 minutes trying to decide where to start. By then the motivation window was smaller, and it felt like work just to begin.

I didn't want another dashboard. I wanted someone to just tell me what to do — once, briefly, before the day got complicated.

So I built ONE THING. It's an email product. Every morning at 8am, it sends one short email with one concrete action per category you've selected (things like work projects, physical health, or something personal you've been deferring). Each action is designed to take 5 to 15 minutes. No streaks, no feed, no check-in required.

I've been using it myself for a few weeks and the main thing I notice is that I stop stalling on starts. Not because I'm more motivated — just because the decision is already made.

It's a paid product ($5–$15/month depending on how many categories). I'm sharing here because the people I've learned most from about this stuff tend to be in this community, and I wanted to hear honest reactions before I market it harder.

Link if you're curious: https://onestep.nanocorp.app

Happy to answer questions about how the action library works or what went into building it.
