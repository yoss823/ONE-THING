# ONE THING MVP Product Brief

## Goal

Ship a paid email product that helps busy adults build momentum by removing daily decision friction. The MVP should prove that people will pay for a tightly scoped daily guidance product before we build personalization, apps, or social features.

## Target User

The initial customer is a busy professional aged 25-45 who wants to improve a few parts of life, likes the idea of self-improvement, and consistently fails when faced with too many options. They are willing to pay for clarity and consistency, not for content depth.

This user:

- checks email every morning
- wants practical actions, not theory
- can reliably make 5-15 minutes available
- prefers a simple system over a flexible one

## Core User Journey

1. User lands on the site and understands the promise in under 10 seconds: "one email each morning with the next small action to take."
2. User subscribes to a single paid plan.
3. User selects 1-3 categories during onboarding.
4. User confirms their email and timezone.
5. At 8:00am in their local timezone, user receives one email containing exactly one action for each selected category.
6. User opens the email, picks one action to do immediately, and can mark each action as done from the email.
7. User can change categories or cancel from their account settings.

## Daily Email Promise

For MVP, ONE THING promises:

> Every day at 8:00am local time, you will receive one email with exactly one clear action for each category you selected, and every action will be doable in 5-15 minutes.

Each action block in the email must include:

- a verb-first title
- a one-sentence instruction
- an estimated time
- a short reason it matters
- a single "Done" action link

MVP constraint:

- users may select up to 3 categories
- the email contains no long-form lesson, feed, or multiple options per category
- if content is unavailable for a category on a given day, the system must still send a fallback action rather than skip the email

## Initial Category Set

Launch with 6 categories only:

1. Mental clarity
2. Organization
3. Health / Energy
4. Work / Business
5. Personal projects
6. Relationships

Rationale:

- broad enough to appeal to mainstream consumers
- concrete enough to generate short daily actions
- practical without relying on motivational copy or therapy framing
- avoids regulated or high-risk advice categories

Categories explicitly excluded from MVP:

- finance
- mental health therapy
- medical advice
- spirituality
- parenting

## Subscription Assumptions

MVP will launch with one paid plan only:

- `$10/month`
- 7-day free trial
- no annual plan at launch
- no free tier beyond the trial
- one subscription includes up to 3 categories

Assumptions behind this choice:

- one plan reduces decision friction and implementation scope
- the product value is the daily system, not premium feature gating
- a trial is necessary because value is established through repeated use, not a one-time visit

Success signal for pricing:

- users who complete trial onboarding and receive at least 5 emails should convert at a rate high enough to justify paid acquisition testing

## MVP Non-Goals

The MVP will not include:

1. AI-generated personalized actions per user
2. Mobile app or push notifications
3. Community, streak leaderboards, or social accountability features
4. More than one pricing tier
5. Deep content libraries, archives, or educational courses

## Product Decisions To Hold Constant

- ONE THING is an email-first product, not a content platform.
- The product sells consistency and clarity, not customization depth.
- Every user should understand what to do next without opening any other page.
- The implementation should optimize for reliable sending, simple onboarding, and easy category management before anything else.
